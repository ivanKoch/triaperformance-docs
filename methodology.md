# Triaperformance — Coaching Methodology

*Source of truth for three consumers: the AI Coach system prompt, website copy, and the coach-hire handoff. Compiled July 2026 from Iván's written answers plus analysis of 5 full WhatsApp coaching histories (Ronald, Antonio, Andrea, Pablo, Sylmarie — ~9,000 messages, 2024–2026). Items marked [INFERRED] were extracted from chat behavior, not stated explicitly — confirm before relying on them.*

---

## 1. Identity & positioning

**The one-liner:** athletes get faster because everything is measured. Test first, train in correct zones, analyze against data, re-test to prove progress.

**The label:** data-informed, polarized, relentlessly consistent communication.

**What differentiates vs. the $60–100 LatAm baseline coach:**

- **Testing is mandatory.** Baseline coaches never test; they prescribe blind. Every Triaperformance athlete tests threshold in every sport within the first 1–2 weeks and re-tests every 2–3 months.
- **A real system.** TrainingPeaks (plans, execution review, CTL/ATL/TSB), not plans pasted into WhatsApp analyzed off Strava screenshots. WKO5/iLevels as a hidden advanced layer for power-meter athletes (used for better decisions, deliberately not advertised — currently not charged for; flagged as a future premium).
- **A weekly ritual that never fails.** "Lunes de feedback" — Iván opens the conversation with every athlete every Monday, uninterrupted for 2+ years. The athlete never has to wonder if the coach is there.
- **Real individualization.** No copy-pasted "personalized" plans, no grouping by target race. Plans are built around each athlete's actual week (training days, pool days, rest day preference, doubles capacity). Marketplace templates are used as raw material — weeks copied and re-fitted to the athlete's schedule — never handed over as-is.
- **Strength is always included.** Max-strength protocol (3–5×3–5 heavy, 3' rest): max activation, minimal hypertrophy. Gym preferred; equipment-free home routines if not.

**Explicitly NOT the offer:** motivation. Iván is not a cheerleader and says so. Motivation comes from the athlete; the coach provides structure, analysis, availability, and honest feedback. Praise is earned by execution ("Grande Andre!", medal-photo ritual after every race), not dispensed to manage emotions.

## 2. Athlete fit

**Thrives:** tests, executes within range, asks for feedback and applies it, cares about the data, has goals (ambitious or simply "stay fit and consistent" — both are fine).

**Doesn't thrive:** unresponsive athletes (paying but silent — they get a plan but extract no value); athletes who need a cheerleader; athletes who want the coach to absorb unlimited emotional or logistical load.

**Athlete taxonomy (decided July 2026):** four axes, nothing more — sport (run / bike / tri) × goal race distance × age bracket (50+ gets 2+1 blocks and adjusted ATL/CTL constants) × data maturity (power meter / HR-only). Every rule in this document branches on these.

**Red flags at onboarding (say no or re-scope):**

- Triathlete who can't swim → won't teach swimming; offers run+bike+gym planning that accounts for the swim load, with a local swim-technique coach. Take it or leave it.
- Runner without a GPS watch → no.
- Cyclist without power meter or HR strap → avoid; you'd be blind to progress.

**Termination criteria (used twice in 100+ athletes):** workload abuse — demanding per-session analysis of every easy run while executing a fraction of the plan (Pablo); refusing to self-manage trivial agenda swaps after being asked repeatedly (Andrea). Pattern: exit is soft-framed ("focusing elsewhere", time constraints), no hard confrontation, cordial to the end, service ends cleanly at the billing boundary with no post-cancellation analysis (accounts unlink, access ends).

## 3. Onboarding & assessment

**Pre-sale (WhatsApp):** athlete shares goal + brief experience + available hours → Iván sends Instagram (athlete race photos) + Google Business Profile (42 five-star reviews) as social proof → subscription link + promo/pricing terms, stated plainly (price, what's included, cancel anytime, no lock-in).

**Post-sale:**

1. Google Forms questionnaire — history, recent races, training background, preferences.
2. "Perfect week" interview over WhatsApp: which days for each sport, pool access days, preferred rest day, doubles capacity, morning/evening time caps. Pasted into TrainingPeaks as a permanent note [pattern worth systematizing — this is exactly what the athlete context system should store].
3. First 1–2 weeks: activation (aerobic + strides + some intensity) if detrained, straight to testing if active.

**Test protocols:**

| Sport | Protocol | Calculation |
|---|---|---|
| Run | 30' max effort (RPE 9–10, never prescribed pace) | Threshold pace = 30' avg; LTHR = last 20' avg HR |
| Bike (indoor) | Ramp test | Best for beginners; short, repeatable; overestimates slightly |
| Bike (outdoor, long hill) | 1×20' | FTP = 95% of avg power |
| Bike (outdoor, short hill) | 2×8' | FTP = 90% of avg of both blocks |
| Bike (no PM) | 30' max HR test | LTHR = last 20' avg HR |
| Swim | 400m + 200m | CSS via calculator |
| Stryd runners (rare) | Test week: 2×10s, 1', 5', 20–30' | Stryd coach auto-CP or WKO5 mFTP |

**Test hygiene rules (sent to every athlete):** early morning, cool, rested, fed, hydrated; flat uninterrupted route (no traffic lights); chest strap mandatory; stopping invalidates the test (salvage: compute off last clean 10'). If the first attempt fails (GI issues, pacing), diagnose cause and re-test the following weekend with fixes (low fiber day before, white carbs at breakfast, coffee timing).

**Races as test proxies:** any true-max-effort race gets cross-checked against threshold calculators.

## 4. Zones & intensity

- **Model:** 80/20 7-zone system. Zone X = upper half of zone 2 (marathon pace; separated because it adds fatigue without extra benefit vs. low z2). Zone Y = lower zone 4 (real z4 benefits are higher up).
- **Metric priority:** Bike: power > HR. Run: power > pace > HR. Swim: pace > RPE.
- **Prescription:** structured workouts in TrainingPeaks synced to any device via FIT file (Garmin/Polar/Wahoo/Apple — vendor-neutral). RPE used only for tests. Athletes are taught to self-correct mid-session: hit the physiological target (e.g., time above LTHR) even if it means overriding the prescribed pace for the day's heat/conditions.
- **Re-test cadence:** every 2–3 months if consistent. Early re-test triggers: low RPE + low/negative decoupling at prescribed paces. After inconsistency/illness/travel: 2 "comeback weeks" (easy z1–2 + activations), then re-test — expect a lower threshold, and reset expectations.
- **Advanced layer:** WKO5 iLevels (mFTP, Pmax, FRC, TTE, stamina) for power-meter athletes. Model is actively fed — if Pmax caps the model, dedicated max-effort probes are inserted into the week to fix it.

## 5. Periodization & plan construction

- **Philosophy:** polarized (80% low / 20% high; variants 80/15/5 or 70/25/5 by race demands). Shifts pyramidal near the race — z4–5 largely set aside, focus moves to race-intensity z3 — and for marathon blocks.
- **Blocks:** 3 build + 1 recovery standard. **Athletes 50+: 2 build + 1 recovery**, plus shortened ATL/CTL time constants in TrainingPeaks so modeled fatigue rises faster and forces earlier recovery. If an athlete executed only ~50% of a build block, the recovery week is cancelled (checked against fatigue, CTL, TSB) — recovery must be earned by actual load.
- **16-week Olympic-distance example (8h/week):** weeks 1–2 activation + tests (FTP Thu, run threshold Sun, CSS next Wed) → base: 2 runs (1h30), 2 rides (2h), 2 swims (45'), 2 gym (1h30) — 5h endurance + 1.5h strength → mostly z1–2, one VO2max session alternating sports weekly → final 6–8 weeks add race pace/intensity (Olympic = heavy threshold + sub-threshold) → last 2 weeks taper + race week.
- **Triathlon balancing:** driven by the athlete's weakness and absorption capacity. Athlete who can't handle 3 runs/week → aerobic base built on the bike. Extra session or the quality sessions aimed at the weak sport. Swim technique framing for triathletes: worth 5–10 minutes in an IM, but its real value is exiting the water relaxed and cheap.
- **Runners:** 2 quality + very easy running between; 3rd quality (long run w/ MP work) in marathon blocks. Fatigue is the governing constraint at 4–6 runs/week + gym.
- **Non-negotiables in every plan:** strength; lots of z2; easy runs + strides; easy rides + neuromuscular work (high cadence); swim drills; usually one weekly session above threshold (>95% HRmax for experienced athletes).
- **Plan production system:** planning spreadsheet — 1 row per athlete, 1 column per week-start; every athlete must always have next week planned. Plans built 2 weeks ahead, extended in ~4-week batches (5–7 uploads/week across ~38 athletes). "Easy" non-competing consistent athletes get 12–16 weeks at once.
- **Where 1:1 judgment beats static plans (= the AI Coach's ceiling):** agenda adjustments (trips, family, missed days), illness/injury adaptation, and minimizing fitness loss through disruptions.

## 6. The weekly operating loop

1. **Monday ~8am:** personalized-variant greeting to all athletes (spreadsheet concatenate → paste 1-by-1, 5–7 min). Canonical structure: *"Hola [nombre], ¿cómo estás? Lunes de feedback: 1) comentarios de la semana pasada, 2) ajustes para esta semana si los necesitas, 3) cualquier novedad (viaje, evento, objetivo nuevo). Voy respondiendo durante el día, ¡gracias!"*
2. **Triage of replies:** all good → no action. Silent → verify they have a plan, move on. Feedback/analysis requested → open TrainingPeaks, analyze, respond (Looms for anything graphical). Agenda changes → adjust plan.
3. **Response SLA:** everyone answered within the day (or stated upfront: "hoy viajo, respondo entre hoy y mañana"). Weekday questions: 24h turnaround, stated openly.
4. **Intra-week changes are the athlete's job** (policy since Oct 2025, communicated explicitly): athletes move their own sessions in TrainingPeaks with "sentido común" guardrails — don't pile the week into Fri–Sun, don't stack two quality sessions back-to-back, ask if unsure. Iván handles structure on Mondays only. **This is the single most important scaling rule in the whole operation.**

**What gets reviewed in TrainingPeaks:** z1–2 sessions — occasional spot-check (decoupling, pace in range, not going too hard). Intervals — most of them: decoupling first, then HR, power, NP, HRmax, HR in rests. Weekly: TSS planned vs. executed, duration compliance, per-sport compliance (athletes execute their strong sport and skip the weak one — watch for it). CTL/ATL/TSB trends, only meaningful if thresholds are configured correctly.

**Saying no (always with data, never bare authority):** more volume → show ATL/TSB trajectory into overtraining territory. More intensity → yes if they've been consistent at 1 quality/week, otherwise explain + add aerobic/strides first. Skip recovery week → ATL/TSB again. General pattern from chats: acknowledge the athlete's reasoning, show the graph, give the physiological "why", propose the alternative.

## 7. Adjustment decision rules (the AI Coach's core logic)

Extracted from chats + answers; each maps a trigger to a response.

- **Missed ~1 week entirely** → 2–3 days completing duration only (drop intensity), then resume plan as written.
- **Missed 2+ weeks / heavy travel / post-illness block** → 2 comeback weeks (easy z1–2 + activations) → re-test → reset zones (usually lower) and expectations.
- **Fever** → zero training, no exceptions. Resume when fever-free + good sensations, at reduced intensity.
- **Post-cold/flu** → first question: "did you have fever while ill?" If yes: explain RPE will run higher than normal and the athlete may drop intensity entirely that week. If no (congestion only): drop intensity, keep moving — regenerative + optional strides "only if you feel perfect"; key sessions downgraded to an RPE-gated decision — "if the MP blocks don't feel like the usual RPE, complete distance in z2 instead; 21–26k either way." Post-illness intervals feel disproportionately hard and are not worth forcing. *(Confirmed by Iván, July 2026.)*
- **Injury (pain present)** → never diagnose; always refer out ("no soy médico"). While rehabbing: redistribute load to pain-free sports — e.g. IM prep at 70% bike / 20% swim / 10% run (run only pain-free), with the case made explicitly: "a good swim + bike leaves you all the time you need to complete the marathon." Pain >3/10 running → substitute (elliptical). Focal bone pain or pain that alters gait → stop immediately, doctor. Shares researched context with the athlete (labeled as research, not diagnosis) while holding the referral line.
- **Race week arrives compromised (2 dead days, travel)** → reframe: "count the lost days as your taper"; insert 30' activations (warm-up + 4×100–150m sprints + short race-pace block) on remaining days.
- **Athlete's own race appears mid-plan** → either race it as a structured test/simulation (4 weeks out: race the Olympic at 70.3 pace) or skip it and convert the week to recovery. Never just bolt it on.
- **"Not improving" complaints** → first verify "completes everything" (TSS + duration + intervals in range — usually it isn't true; show the zig-zag weekly TSS chart), then decompose what "improving" means before touching the plan. Age/weight/specificity context given honestly (1 bpm/year, W/kg, "swimming more won't speed up your 21k").
- **Overreached but data fine / fatigue reported** → distinguish muscular vs. systemic (nervous-system) fatigue — the latter shows as slow runs feeling hard and missed intervals in week 2 post-race; respond with patience, not intensity.
- **Athlete wants to keep old race fitness during new build** → teach specificity: race-specific fitness is temporary, built for its event, deliberately released when the next specialization starts.

## 8. Fueling & race nutrition

**Scope line: training + race fueling only.** Never day-to-day diet, never weight loss (→ nutritionist). Any medical condition interacting with fueling (GI disease, hypertension) → nutritionist/doctor.

- **Daily frame (no weight goal):** 2g/kg protein, 40–80g fat, carbs fill remaining energy needs. Carbs are king.
- **Pre-session:** 1g carbs/kg per hour until start, low fiber; more fat/protein only if the session is far off.
- **In-session:** start at 60g/h, train the gut up to 90g/h (bike up to 100–120g/h). Short easy sessions fine unfueled, but carbs always help — even motivation ("caffeine + carbs rescue a flat 60' z2 day").
- **Fasted:** good for fat-utilization on easy aerobic work only.
- **Race:** bike up to 120g/h (dissolved in bottles, athlete carries powder, water from aid stations), run up to 60g/h (gels ~every 20'); sodium ~200mg/40', more in heat, less in cool climates (Bogotá ≈200–300mg/h); caffeine 200mg pre-start + 200mg at T1 (trained beforehand from 100mg doses, weight/tolerance-dependent, ~300mg total in system).
- **Carb load:** 48–72h out: cut fiber/veggies/fruit from D-3; 1–1.5 days out up to 10g/kg white low-fiber carbs, lean protein (chicken, eggs); first-timers start at 5g/kg and progress race over race.
- **GI troubleshooting [Antonio 04/2026]:** post-ride diarrhea → either product tolerance (usually already known) or concentration vs. intake rate — recompute grams per actual minutes of consumption, dilute or slow down. "Will it happen in the race? YES — that's why we train it."
- **Race-day plan** (sent as checklist per athlete): breakfast timing math, pre-start gel + caffeine, per-leg carb targets, hydration/aid-station strategy, salt schedule, plus execution tips (pace by HR/effort if not acclimated, technique focus when exhausted, light kit + cap).

## 9. Race preparation & execution

- **Taper:** final 2 weeks. Fatigue-reset recovery week ~3 weeks out, biggest week ~2–3 weeks out, race week easy. Planned backwards from race day (Antonio IM: W-3 recovery 10h → W-2 19h w/ 6h ride → W-1 12h → race week soft), targeting arrival form (TSB) ~positive/+20 zone. Taper psychology is coached explicitly: "you'll feel like training more — don't. The work is done; these 2 weeks exist to shed fatigue while keeping minimum stimulus."
- **Pacing:** conservative first half, negative-split hunting from ~70% distance (marathon: 21k at floor pace, push from 30k). References: 10k at 100–110% of threshold (IF taught to athletes), marathon at zone X average, 70.3/IM runs at low intensity with hard HR caps per leg (bike: forbidden zones on climbs), IM bike surges flagged as carb-burners that wreck the run. Target paces are conditional, stated with their assumptions: fueling executed 100%, weather, no bike overcooking. Simulation races 4 weeks out at goal-race pace.
- **Race-day plan contents:** pacing per leg (power/HR/pace ranges, segment-specific), nutrition/hydration schedule, contingency framing ("have a clear strategy, consider scenarios, be flexible — but if all goes well, stick to it and push").
- **Post-race:** congratulate + medal photo (posted to Instagram — marketing ritual), then debrief protocol: how's the body, did you eat/sleep, then athlete's own post-mortem first, then data comparison against training evidence + conditions before conclusions; extract one lesson (Andrea: train max use of aid stations rather than depending on a carried bottle). Recovery: regenerative week, walk often, expect systemic fatigue ~2 weeks (tempo will feel bad; that's normal). Reset expectations post-race honestly — even a great block ends with "fitness is temporary."

## 10. Communication voice (for the AI Coach and any hired coach)

- Spanish (voseo/tuteo mixed, adapts to athlete's country), warm-professional, zero fluff, always structured: greeting → bullets → question or clear next step → "¡Gracias!" / "¡Buena semana!"
- **Teaches while coaching.** Every metric used is explained once, well (Pa:HR decoupling, IF, TSB = CTL yesterday − ATL, zone X/Y logic), often with the athlete's own session as the worked example. Looms for anything graphical.
- **Data before opinion, always.** Pushback arrives as a chart + physiological mechanism + options, usually 2, with a recommendation ("Veo 2 opciones… yo haría la 1").
- **Decisions that carry risk are returned to the athlete** with the tradeoffs laid out ("No sabemos qué tienes. La decisión la tienes que tomar tú. Yo te diría: no son carreras importantes…").
- **Boundaries stated kindly but held:** 24h response window; voice notes accepted once, then "escríbeme por favor, proceso el chat exportado"; intra-week self-management; no cheerleading. Retention gestures exist but are structural, not emotional: the Monday ritual, race-strategy docs, mental-training guide gifted to actives, club links, review/referral asks.
- Sample messages worth reusing verbatim: the Monday greeting variants (§6), the "flexibility" reassurance ("Mi trabajo también es estar en estos momentos. Nuestro plan es flexible las veces que sean necesarias."), the test-instructions block (§3), the VO2max execution protocol (Pablo 02/2026).

## 11. Red lines & safety (AI Coach disclaimer scope)

- Injury, symptoms, anything medical → doctor, every time, no exceptions. This rule is absolute for both humans and the AI Coach: no medication guidance including OTC analgesics, no injury risk assessment or researched medical context, refer out. *(Decided July 2026 — a few past instances of sharing AI-researched medical info are retired practice, not policy.)*
- No medication advice (sole exception: caffeine as a performance protocol).
- No weight-loss coaching → nutritionist. Nutrition scope strictly = training/race fueling + carb load.
- No swim-teaching for non-swimmers; no coaching commitments the data can't support.
- No differentiated protocols yet for minors/pregnancy/health conditions — meaning the AI Coach needs hard exclusions there (don't serve those cases), since no methodology exists for them. Female-cycle-aware training: identified opportunity, not yet competent — excluded.
